Cleaning Agents

Soaps

A common detergent made from fats or fatty acids and an alkali like sodium hydroxide. Soaps contain surfactants, which reduce the surface tension of water.

Detergents

Break up dirt and soil, making it easier to wash away. Synthetic detergents can work in any water condition.

Degreasers

Also known as solvent cleaners, degreasers remove grease from surfaces like counters, oven tops, and grill backsplashes.

Abrasives

Used to clean dirt from hard surfaces like pots, pans, and floors. Abrasives are effective for removing stubborn stains and spots. However, they can scratch some materials, like mirrors.

Oven cleaners

Traditional oven cleaners contain sodium hydroxide (lye), solvents, and other ingredients,[3] They work best when used in a slightly warm (not hot) oven. If used in a self-cleaning oven, the lye can cause permanent damage to the oven.

All-Purpose cleaners

All-purpose cleansers contain mixtures of anionic and nonionic surfactants, polymeric phosphates or other sequestering agents, solvents, hydrotropic substances, polymeric compounds, corrosion inhibitors, skin-protective agents, and sometimes perfumes and colorants.[4] Aversive agents, such as denatonium, are occasionally added to cleaning products to discourage animals and small children from consuming them.

Bleach

Bleach is a powerful cleaning agent commonly used for disinfecting and whitening purposes. It primarily contains sodium hypochlorite or hydrogen peroxide as its active ingredient.

Equipment Cleaner

Equipment cleaners are specialized cleaning products designed to effectively clean and maintain kitchen and industrial equipment, such as appliances, utensils, cookware, and machinery. These cleaners help ensure optimal performance, hygiene, and longevity of equipment by removing dirt, grease, food residues, and other contaminants.

Surface Cleaner

Surface cleaners are versatile cleaning products designed to effectively clean a variety of surfaces in homes, kitchens, and workplaces. These cleaners are formulated to remove dirt, grease, grime, and bacteria, ensuring a hygienic and spotless environment.
